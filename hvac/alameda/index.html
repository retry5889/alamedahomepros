Why Alameda Homes Need Good HVAC

Alameda's island microclimate is milder than inland East Bay cities, but that doesn't mean HVAC is optional. Older homes often have:

  • Aging furnaces — many built in the 1970s–90s, past their efficient lifespan
  • No air conditioning — older Alameda homes weren't built with AC, but summer temperatures are rising
  • Leaky ductwork — decades of settling in older homes creates gaps and disconnections
  • Poor insulation — pre-1980 construction often lacks modern insulation standards

Heat pumps are increasingly popular in Alameda because the mild climate makes them highly efficient year-round. Federal and California state rebates can offset 30–50% of installation costs.
