Why Electrical Work Matters in the East Bay

East Bay homes built before 1980 — 72.5% of Alameda's housing stock — often have 60 to 100-amp electrical panels and outdated wiring that can't handle modern demands. Adding an EV charger, heat pump, or kitchen renovation to an undersized panel is a safety risk and a code violation.

Panel upgrades are the most common electrical project in the East Bay, driven by California's electrification push and the rapid adoption of electric vehicles. A qualified electrician can assess your current system, pull the right permits, and bring your home up to code.

Every electrician on Alameda HomePro holds an active C-10 Electrical Contractor license from the California CSLB. We limit each category to 3–5 providers.

Common Electrical Services

  • Panel Upgrades — upgrade from 60/100A to 200A for modern loads
  • Whole-House Rewiring — replace knob-and-tube or aluminum wiring
  • EV Charger Installation — Level 2 (240V) home charging stations
  • Code Compliance — inspections and corrections for older systems
  • Lighting — recessed lighting, fixtures, landscape lighting
  • Whole-House Surge Protection — protect electronics and appliances
  • Outlet & Switch Work — GFCI outlets, smart switches, dedicated circuits
