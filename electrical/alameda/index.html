Why Alameda Homes Need Electrical Upgrades

Alameda's older homes face a growing electrical demand gap:

  • Undersized panels — 60–100A panels were standard before 1980 but can't handle EV chargers, heat pumps, and modern appliances simultaneously
  • Outdated wiring — some pre-1950 homes still have knob-and-tube or early Romex wiring that doesn't meet current code
  • EV adoption — Alameda has one of the highest EV adoption rates in the East Bay, driving demand for Level 2 home chargers
  • Electrification mandates — California's building electrification push is accelerating the shift from gas to electric, requiring panel and circuit capacity

Every electrician on this page holds an active C-10 license and knows Alameda's permitting process. They'll assess your panel, plan the work, and handle permits.
